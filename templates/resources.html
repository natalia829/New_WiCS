Summer Opportunities

Internships

Google: STEP

Google's STEP program is a developmental opportunity for first- and second-year undergraduate students with a passion for technology—especially students from historically underrepresented groups in the field.

Google: Software Engineering Internship

This internship is intended for students with experience in Data Structures or Algorithms.

Explore Microsoft

Explore Microsoft is a 12-week summer internship program specifically designed for first and second year college students.

Facebook University

Facebook University is a hands-on, immersive internship program that enables students from underrepresented communities to get to know Facebook’s people, products and services. In just eight weeks, the program gives interns across engineering, analytics, product design, operations and global marketing solutions roles the opportunity to make a real impact and help redefine how the world connects.

Twitter Academy

Twitter offers 12-week fall, spring, or summer internships all over the world.

Coding it Forward

This is internship program for innovative students to solve pressing problems in federal agencies. Civic Digital Fellows are mission-driven software engineers, data scientists, product managers, and designers who spend their summers innovating at the intersection of technology and public service.

Research

Undergraduate Research Opporunities at Harvard

Find out more about the undergraduate research opportunities in Computer Science here at Harvard.

MIT Summer Research Program

This is a nine-week, fully funded summer program that seeks to bring together talented underrepresented minorities and underserved students to engage in on-capmus research led by MIT faculty, grad students, and postdoctoral fellows.

Distributed Research Experiences for Undergraduates (DREU)

DREU is a highly selective program that aims to increase the number of students from underrepresented groups (including women, minorities, or persons with disabilities) interested in Computer Science by matching them with a faculty mentor for a summer research experience at the faculty mentor’s home institution.
